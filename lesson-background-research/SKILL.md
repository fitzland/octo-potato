# Research Lesson Context

This skill is designed to provide rich, expository background information to supplement Bible study preparation. It moves beyond simple definitions to provide the "why" and "so what" of biblical context.

## Workflow

### 1. Identify the Subject
Clarify the specific entity or concept the user is inquiring about. If the user mentions a specific scripture passage (e.g., 2 Corinthians 8:1-9), use that as the anchor for the research.

### 2. Gather Information
Use external research (e.g., Biblehub chapter summaries or cross-references) and internal knowledge to gather details across three dimensions:
- **Historical Context**: Dates, political climate, geography, and specific events (e.g., the poverty and persecution of the Macedonian churches).
- **Cultural/Social Context**: Social structures, Greco-Roman or Jewish customs, and audience expectations.
- **Theological Significance**: How this topic connects to the broader biblical narrative or the specific message of the book.

### 3. Connect to the Lesson
Don't just provide a "fact sheet." Explicitly connect the findings back to the user's lesson goal:
- **Impact on Interpretation**: How does this change how we read the text?
- **Pedagogical Use**: Suggest how this information could be used in a "Context & Insights" section or as a "Discussion Question."
- **Illustration/Metaphor**: Provide a modern parallel if appropriate.

## Output Format

### [Subject Name]
**Historical/Cultural Context**. Provide a concise but rich summary of the facts.

**Connection to [Scripture Reference]**. Explain why this matters for the current study.

**Teaching Insight**. Suggest a specific way to include this in the lesson (e.g., a "Did you know?" bullet or a discussion prompt).
