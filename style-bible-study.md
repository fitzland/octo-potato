# Bible Study Style Guide

This document defines the structural and stylistic standards for all adult Bible study lessons.

## 1. Core Teaching Philosophy: The Socratic Arc
Every lesson must move through three distinct phases to engage the whole person:
1.  **Engaging the Mind (Observe & Interpret):** Focus on what the text says and why it matters. Use "Clustered Socratic Flow."
2.  **Engaging the Affections (Heart Check):** Focus on internal motives, desires, and emotional responses to the truth.
3.  **Engaging the Will (Decision & Challenges):** Focus on concrete obedience and practical application.

## 2. Clustered Socratic Flow (Mind Section)
Instead of separating all observations from all interpretations, pair them in logical clusters:
- **The "What" (Observation):** Establish the facts of the text.
- **The "Why/How" (Interpretation):** Immediately follow with a question that explores the significance, logic, or principle behind those facts.

**Formatting for Questions:**
- Use a **Bold Keyword** at the start of a question cluster (e.g., `> **The List.**`).
- Use *Italics* for thematic emphasis or "linchpin" words within questions.
- Provide brief, bracketed answers `[like this]` immediately following the relevant question.

## 3. Structural Standards

### Header
- Use standard Markdown frontmatter for `tags` and `title`.
- Include a `# Title` and the scripture reference.

### Series Overview
- Include a table showing the lesson's place in the broader series.

### Introduction
- **Icebreaker:** One or two questions to hook the audience.
- **Synthesis:** A brief paragraph connecting the icebreaker to the biblical context.
- **Core Message:** A one-sentence "Big Idea" for the lesson.

### Section Breakdown
Each passage section should follow this pattern:
1.  **Scripture Block:** Blockquote the text (usually BSB version).
2.  **Engaging the Mind:** Paired What/Why questions with bracketed answers.
3.  **Engaging the Affections:** Reflection questions focused on the heart.
4.  **Transition:** A brief sentence leading into the next section.

### Closing
- **The Synthesis/Analogy:** A final wrap-up thought (e.g., "The Polished Shield").
- **Engaging the Will (Decision):** Bulleted commitments (e.g., "Will you...").
- **Challenges (Practice):** Concrete, measurable actions for the week.
- **Memory Verse:** Specific verse to memorize.
- **Prayer:** A structured prayer closing the session.

## 4. Formatting & Tone
- **Tone:** Senior software engineer meets expository Bible teacher—precise, logical, yet pastoral.
- **Bible Version:** Default to Berean Standard Bible (BSB) unless otherwise noted.
- **Markdown:** Use standard GitHub-flavored Markdown. Use `>` for all discussion questions.
