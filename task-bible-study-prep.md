# Task: Prepare a New Lesson

This document defines the process for creating a rich, expository, discussion-driven Bible study from a scripture passage.

## Core Objective
Create a lesson that leads the student from the text (**Mind**) to the motives (**Heart**) and finally to the mission (**Will**), using a Socratic questioning method that facilitates discovery.

## Preparation Workflow

### 1. Research & Analysis
- **Observe:** Identify the primary actors, actions, and theological themes in the passage.
- **Interpret:** Determine the author's original intent and the timeless principles presented.
- **Synthesize:** Draft the "Core Message" (Big Idea) for the lesson.

### 2. Drafting the Lesson
Follow the structural and formatting rules in [style-bible-study.md](./style-bible-study.md).

#### Section 1: Engaging the Mind (Observe & Interpret)
- **Use Clustered Socratic Flow:** Pair questions that establish facts (**What**) with questions that explore significance (**Why/How**).
- **Format:** Use a **Bold Keyword** to start each question cluster.
- **Answers:** Provide brief, bracketed answers for each question.

#### Section 2: Engaging the Affections (Heart Check)
- **Focus:** Shift from what the text says to how it exposes our hearts.
- **Questioning:** Draft 2-3 reflection questions that challenge internal motives, desires, or fears related to the passage.

#### Section 3: Engaging the Will (Decision & Practice)
- **Decision:** Draft clear, actionable "Will you..." commitments.
- **Challenges:** Provide concrete practices (the "Homework") that help the student live out the truth.

### 3. Finalization & Formatting
- **Audit:** Ensure all questions use the `>` blockquote format.
- **Style:** Ensure italics are used for "linchpin" terms and thematic emphasis.
- **Consistency:** Verify that the lesson fits the broader series (if applicable).

## Technical Requirements
- Use Markdown for all documentation.
- Save final lessons in the `./docs` folder.
- Use the filename format: `topic-sXX-reference-notes.md` (e.g., `character-s06-1pet03-integrity-notes.md`).
